# Revenue growth strategy

## When this applies
"How do we grow revenue X%," "we've plateaued, how do we reaccelerate,"
general growth-strategy mandates without a specific lever named yet.

## Core tree
```
Growth = (More customers x same spend)
       + (Same customers x more spend per customer: price, cross-sell, frequency)
       + (New markets/segments/geographies)
       + (New products/lines)
       (+ M&A, if organic options are insufficient — route to ma-acquisition.md)
```

## Diagnostic sequence
1. **Where is growth coming from today, and where has it stalled?** —
   Decompose current growth (or lack of it) by customer acquisition vs.
   expansion vs. retention/churn, and by segment/geography/product, before
   proposing new levers. Don't recommend a new lever to fix a problem in an
   existing one (e.g. don't chase new markets if the real issue is churn in
   the core business).
2. **Size each candidate lever** — rough TAM/headroom for each plausible
   growth path (penetration in existing segments, expansion/cross-sell,
   new segments, new geos, new products), so the comparison is apples to
   apples rather than enthusiasm-driven.
3. **Check the right to win and cost to pursue** for each candidate lever —
   same logic as market-entry.md when the lever is a new market, and as
   pricing.md when the lever is price/expansion revenue.
4. **Sequence, don't shotgun.** Most companies can execute 1-2 growth
   initiatives well at a time; prioritize by size of opportunity x
   probability of success x speed, and say what's deprioritized and why.

## Common traps
- Proposing new-market or new-product growth without diagnosing why
  existing growth has stalled first.
- Listing every plausible lever without sizing or prioritizing them.
- Ignoring capacity/capability constraints on how many initiatives can
  actually run in parallel.

## Typical recommendation shapes
A prioritized, sequenced set of 1-3 growth initiatives with sizing,
right-to-win rationale, and resourcing implications — not an exhaustive
menu of every theoretically available lever.
