# New market entry

## When this applies
"Should we enter market/country/segment X," "should we launch in a new
geography," "is this adjacent market worth pursuing."

## Core questions, in order
1. **Is the market attractive on its own terms?**
   - Size (TAM/SAM/SOM) and growth rate.
   - Competitive intensity (Porter's Five Forces, lightly applied: rivalry,
     entry barriers, supplier/buyer power, substitutes).
   - Regulatory environment, especially for cross-border entry.
2. **Can we win here?** — Does the company have a right to win: existing
   capability transfer, brand recognition, relevant cost advantage,
   distribution relationships? Attractive markets with no competitive edge
   are traps.
3. **How do we enter?** — Organic build, acquisition, joint venture/
   partnership, licensing. Each has a different cost, speed, risk, and
   control profile — name the tradeoff, don't default to "organic."
4. **What does success cost and how long does it take?** — Entry investment,
   expected payback period, and the realistic ramp curve (most market
   entries underperform initial volume assumptions in year 1-2).
5. **Competitive response** — will incumbents retaliate (price war, blocking
   distribution), and does the entry plan survive that response?

## Common traps
- Confusing "big market" with "attractive market" — size without a right to
  win or without competitive headroom is not sufficient.
- Ignoring entry mode tradeoffs and assuming organic build by default.
- Modeling a straight-line ramp instead of a realistic, usually slower,
  adoption curve.

## Typical recommendation shapes
Enter via a named mode with a stated investment and timeline, enter a
narrower beachhead segment first, partner instead of building, or don't
enter (with the specific attractiveness or right-to-win gap named).
