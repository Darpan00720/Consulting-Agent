# Pricing strategy

## When this applies
"Should we raise/lower price," "how should we price this new product,"
"are we leaving money on the table."

## Core questions, in order
1. **What's the pricing objective?** — Maximize volume/share, maximize
   margin, signal positioning/quality, defend against a competitor. The
   right price depends entirely on which objective is actually in play.
2. **What's the value-based ceiling?** — What is the offering worth to the
   customer (cost savings delivered, revenue enabled, next-best-alternative
   price)? This sets the upper bound independent of cost.
3. **What's the cost-based floor?** — Variable cost at minimum; full cost
   recovery for sustainable pricing. This sets the lower bound.
4. **Where do competitors sit, and how will they respond?** — A price move
   that ignores competitive reaction is incomplete; model at least one
   retaliation scenario for a material price change.
5. **Elasticity** — how sensitive is demand to the price change? If not
   given, state the breakeven elasticity (the elasticity at which the price
   change stops paying off) rather than guessing a point estimate.
6. **Structure, not just level** — would a different pricing structure
   (tiered, usage-based, bundling, segmented/price discrimination) capture
   more value than a uniform price change? Often the bigger lever.

## Common traps
- Cost-plus pricing without checking the value-based ceiling.
- Ignoring competitive response to a price change.
- Jumping to "raise/lower price" without considering structure changes that
  could outperform a simple level change.

## Typical recommendation shapes
A specific price level or range with stated rationale (value/cost/competitive
bounds), a structural change (tiering, bundling, segmentation), or a
test-and-learn rollout plan for an uncertain elasticity case.
