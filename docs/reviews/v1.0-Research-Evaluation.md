# StratAgent v1.0 — Independent Research Evaluation

**Independent AI Research & Evaluation · Evaluation only (no feature work)**
**Version under test:** `0.1.0-rc2` (RC1.2) · **Date:** 2026-07-09 · **Model driving agents:** Claude Opus 4.8

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This report evaluates StratAgent as a management-consulting system using genuine,
reproducible engagement runs, a documented 14-criterion rubric, an artifact-based
ablation, and a live single-pass baseline comparison.

**Headline results (all from genuinely executed runs — nothing fabricated):**

- **StratAgent mean quality: 7.6 / 10** across 3 genuine engagements (range 7.0–8.4).
- **Single-pass baseline mean: 5.5 / 10** across 2 genuine baselines.
- **The ~2.1-point gap is concentrated in governance (9 vs 1), confidence
  calibration (8–9 vs 3–4), quantification/financial reasoning (7–8 vs 3–5), and
  evidence traceability — not in the headline recommendation, where the
  single-pass model is competitive.**
- **Zero hallucinations** observed across all 5 runs (3 StratAgent + 2 baseline).
- **The governance layer demonstrably works:** in a controlled comparison the
  single-pass baseline shipped the *exact* overconfident claim ("$250M cost-out
  is achievable") that StratAgent's Challenger caught, returned `needs_rework`
  on, and corrected to "~$200M base; $250M is a stretch; ~$155–183M floor if the
  union bars closure."

**Where StratAgent provides measurable value:** governance (catching
overconfidence, blindspots, and cross-analyst inconsistency), calibration
discipline, quantification, and end-to-end traceability. **Where it does not
add much over a good single-pass model:** report readability and, often, the
core strategic recommendation itself.

**Verdict: READY FOR LIMITED BETA.** The system produces defensible,
well-governed, hallucination-free output with measurable value over a baseline —
suitable for human-supervised use — but the empty evidence base (every number is
a labeled assumption), small validation sample (n=3), non-determinism, and the
fact that only 1 of 3 engagements ran on the current mandatory-gate pipeline
preclude an unsupervised production claim.

## 2. Methodology

- **System under test:** StratAgent 0.1.0-rc2 — the live `solve-case`
  orchestration dispatching real specialist subagents, with RC1.2's mandatory
  governance gates and live validation gate.
- **Genuine execution only.** Each engagement is a real multi-subagent LLM
  session (~11–13 dispatches). Three were executed end-to-end (one full pipeline,
  two lightweight incl. a real rework loop). No run, score, or statistic in this
  report is synthetic.
- **Scope decision (documented).** A literal full matrix — 12 archetypes ×
  (full + 4 ablations + 3 baselines) genuinely run — is ~1,000 subagent
  dispatches across many sessions; fabricating any of it would be the exact
  failure this evaluation exists to detect. By explicit agreement the campaign:
  (a) reuses the 3 existing genuine engagements; (b) runs 2 genuine single-pass
  baselines (cheap, high-signal); (c) reconstructs the ablation from real
  artifacts and a natural experiment. New full engagements were **not** run.
- **Scoring.** Independent 14-criterion rubric (§4), 0–10, with written
  justification per criterion. Execution and scoring are separated; scoring is an
  adversarial critical pass.
- **Baselines.** Fresh, isolated `general-purpose` agents answered two cases in a
  single CoT pass — no team, no governance, no retrieval — so they cannot be
  contaminated by StratAgent artifacts in the evaluator's context.

## 3. Threats to Validity

1. **Small n.** 3 StratAgent engagements, 2 baselines. Descriptive statistics
   only; **no confidence intervals are computed** — they would be meaningless.
2. **Evaluator/executor share a model family.** StratAgent's agents, the
   baselines, and the scorer are all Claude. Scoring is done adversarially to
   mitigate, but this is not a fully independent judge. **No GPT baseline was
   run** (no access); the single-pass baseline represents "an unstructured
   single-model workflow," not a specific vendor.
3. **Version skew.** Only Northwind ran the full pipeline; the two lightweight
   engagements ran under the *pre-RC1.2* pipeline that skipped the Reviewer.
   Their governance scores reflect that as-run gap (which RC1.2 has since closed).
4. **Non-determinism.** Re-running any engagement would not reproduce byte-identical
   output; conclusions are about the *distribution* of behavior, not a fixed artifact.
5. **Ablation is artifact-based, not a controlled quantitative sweep** (§7).
6. **Case realism.** Benchmark cases are fictional (though realistic); no real
   client ground truth exists to validate recommendations against outcomes.

## 4. Dataset (Phase 1)

The canonical benchmark is [v1.0-Validation-Benchmark.md](v1.0-Validation-Benchmark.md)
— **30 cases, all 12 required archetypes**, across manufacturing, technology,
financial services, consumer goods, healthcare, and public sector, at small /
mid-market / enterprise scale. This evaluation refines it with an **ambiguity
tier** per case and adds the 14th rubric criterion.

### Archetype coverage (required 12, all present)
Profitability · Market Entry · Pricing · Growth · M&A · Due Diligence · Cost
Reduction · Digital Transformation · Operations (Supply Chain) · Organizational
Design · Product Strategy · Turnaround. (Customer Experience also covered.)

### Ambiguity tiers (refinement)
- **A1 — bounded:** clear decision + some numbers (e.g. C09 Halberd cost-out).
- **A2 — moderate:** decision clear, key facts missing → load-bearing assumptions
  (e.g. C04 Northwind — no market sizes given).
- **A3 — open/soft:** no numbers, uncovered archetype, political constraints
  (e.g. C20 Harbor & Vine org redesign).
The three genuinely-run engagements deliberately span A1, A2, A3.

### Rubric (14 criteria, 0–10)
Problem framing · Framework selection · MECE quality · Evidence usage · Strategic
reasoning · Financial reasoning · **Operational reasoning** (new axis) · Risk
analysis · Recommendation quality · Implementation roadmap · Report clarity ·
Governance effectiveness · Confidence calibration · Overall consulting quality.
Anchors: 9–10 partner-quality · 6–8 solid consultant · 3–5 junior/flawed · 0–2 failing.

## 6. Failure Analysis (Phase 4)

Taxonomy of failures **observed** (not hypothetical), with root cause and whether
the system caught it.

| # | Failure mode | Instance (observed) | Root cause | Caught by |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F-a | **Overconfidence** | Baseline Halberd: "$250M achievable" as base; C20 analyst confidence 0.7 | LLM analysts over-assert | Challenger (StratAgent); **uncaught in baseline** |
| F-b | **Reasoning inconsistency** | C09: $343M cross-analyst cost-base disagreement | Independent analysts, no shared base | Challenger (late); Reviewer would catch earlier |
| F-c | **Weak issue tree / MECE gap** | C20: missing "coordination-layer" option | Analyst framed 3 options, missed the lightest | Challenger |
| F-d | **Missing evidence** | All 3: every number is a labeled assumption | Vault holds no benchmarks (ADR-003 D-6) | N/A — systemic, honestly labeled |
| F-e | **Governance miss (as-run)** | C20, C09 ran with no Reviewer | pre-RC1.2 lightweight skip | **Fixed in RC1.2** (mandatory gates) |
| F-f | **Blindspot** | C04: CLOUD Act ceiling; incumbent renewal-bundling | Analysts scoped to their branch | Challenger |
| F-g | **Framework-store divergence** | C20: classifier vs selector saw different stores | Two stores | **Fixed in RC1.2** (unified) |
| F-h | **Latency outlier** | C04 risk-analyst ~22 min | Model inference variance | Not a correctness issue |
| — | **Hallucination** | **None observed** in any of 5 runs | Strong labeling discipline | — |

**Root-cause summary:** the dominant *content* failures (over-confidence,
inconsistency, MECE gaps, blindspots) are inherent to LLM analysts and are the
**designed job of the governance layer** — which caught them in every StratAgent
run and left them **uncaught in both baselines**. The *systemic* failure
(missing evidence) is architectural-by-design and now addressable via the
Evidence Provider seam (ADR-007). The two *process* failures (governance miss,
store divergence) were resolved by RC1.2.

## 7. Ablation Results (Phase 5)

**Method note (threat to validity):** this is an **artifact-based** ablation —
reconstructed from real engagement artifacts, a natural experiment, and the
baseline (a de-facto "no-scaffolding" arm) — **not** a controlled quantitative
sweep. Directional, evidence-grounded, not a precise effect size.

| Component removed | Evidence source | Observed effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Challenger** | pre/post-challenge deltas in all 3 + both baselines | **Highest-value component.** Without it: C04 ships without the CLOUD Act caveat and as an "EU NPV winner" (false); C09 ships the $250M over-promise; C20 ships at inflated 0.7 confidence with a missing option. The baselines (no challenger) *empirically confirm this* — Halberd baseline shipped the exact over-promise. |
| **Reviewer** | natural experiment: C20, C09 ran without it | Cross-analyst inconsistency (C09 $343M) and MECE gaps (C20) survived to the Challenger, which caught them **late** (double duty). Reviewer is the earlier, cheaper consistency net; without it, correctness depends entirely on Challenger strength. This evidence is *why* RC1.2 made it mandatory. |
| **Framework selection** | baseline proxy (implicit frameworks) | ~2–3 point drop in MECE quality (7–9 → 5–6) and framework selection (8 → 5). Structure degrades; the answer can still be reached but less rigorously. |
| **Knowledge retrieval** | observed: vault has no benchmarks | Contributes **framework routing, not evidence grounding**, under the current empty-benchmark vault. Removing it would degrade framework selection but lose no evidence (there is none to lose) — a real, slightly counter-intuitive finding tied to F-d. |

**Component-contribution ranking (by observed quality impact):**
Challenger > Reviewer > Framework selection > Knowledge retrieval (current vault).

| Dimension | StratAgent | Single-pass baseline | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mean quality (0–10) | 7.6 | 5.5 | StratAgent +2.1 |
| Core recommendation | Rigorous, mode tied to window | Competent, defensible | **Comparable** |
| Report clarity | 8–9 | 8 | **Comparable** |

| Governance | Reviewer + Challenger + rework | None | StratAgent categorically ahead |
| Calibration | Nothing >0.6; challenger corrects | Overconfident by omission | StratAgent clearly ahead |

| Blindspot catch | CLOUD Act ceiling caught | Missed | StratAgent ahead |
| Overconfidence guard | $250M flagged as stretch | Shipped $250M as base | StratAgent ahead |

**Where StratAgent provides measurable value:** governance, calibration,
quantification, traceability, and blindspot/overconfidence catching. **Where it
does not:** headline recommendation quality and readability are close, at ~10×
the cost and latency. The value proposition is *auditable, de-risked* consulting
— not a better one-liner.

## 9. Statistics (Phase 7)

**Small-n disclaimer (repeated):** n=3 StratAgent, n=2 baseline. Descriptive only.
No confidence intervals — they would be statistically meaningless at this n.

- **StratAgent overall:** mean **7.6**, min 7.0, max 8.4, spread 1.4. Tight.
- **Baseline overall:** mean **5.5**, min 5.4, max 5.6.
- **Gap:** +2.1 points, StratAgent.
- **Per-criterion gap (StratAgent − baseline mean), largest first:**
  Governance +7.5 · Confidence calibration +4.5 · Financial reasoning +3.5 ·
  Evidence usage +2 · MECE +2.5 · Overall +1.7 · Risk +2 · Operational +1.5 ·
  Report clarity ≈0 · Recommendation ≈+0.5 · Roadmap ≈+1.
- **Distribution of failures caught vs. shipped:** StratAgent caught 100% of
  observed content failures (over-confidence, inconsistency, MECE gap, blindspot)
  in its runs; baselines shipped 2/2 of the comparable failures uncaught.
- **Hallucination frequency:** 0 / 5 runs.
- **Best case:** C04 Northwind (8.4). **Worst case:** C20 Harbor & Vine (7.0) —
  soft, uncovered archetype, no Reviewer.

## 10. Recommendations

1. **Attach an Evidence Provider before any production claim.** The single
   biggest limitation is F-d (every number is an assumption). The seam exists
   (ADR-007); populate at least one sourced provider for benchmarks/comps.
2. **Re-validate the two lightweight cases under RC1.2** so their governance
   scores reflect the now-mandatory Reviewer (expected to raise C20/C09 governance
   and consistency).
3. **Keep the Challenger central** — it is the highest-value component by a wide
   margin and the single clearest differentiator from a baseline.
4. **Human-in-the-loop for beta** — because outputs are non-deterministic and
   assumption-heavy, a reviewer should sign off before client delivery.
5. **Do not market a "better recommendation"** — market *auditable, governed,
   calibrated* consulting; that is what the evidence supports.

## 11. Future Research

- A **controlled quantitative ablation** (re-run cases with each component
  disabled) to replace the artifact-based one here.
- A **larger genuine sample** (n≥12, one per archetype) for real distributional
  statistics and per-archetype quality.
- A **non-Claude judge and a GPT baseline** to remove the shared-model threat.
- **Outcome validation** against real engagements with known results.
- **Determinism study:** run the same case k times, measure recommendation and
  score variance.

## 13. Final Verdict

> ## READY FOR LIMITED BETA

**Supported by:** mean 7.6/10 across 3 genuine engagements spanning A1–A3
ambiguity; a governance layer that caught 100% of observed content failures and a
measurable +2.1 quality gap over a single-pass baseline that shipped the exact
overconfidence StratAgent corrected; **zero hallucinations** in 5 runs; honest
calibration and full traceability.

**Precluding "Ready for Production":** every quantitative claim is an unbacked
assumption (empty evidence base); n=3 is far too small for a production quality
guarantee; outputs are non-deterministic; and only 1 of 3 engagements ran on the
current mandatory-gate pipeline.

**Not "Needs Architectural Changes":** the architecture inconsistencies from the
prior campaign were resolved in RC1.2 (unified framework store, live validation
gate, mandatory gates, evidence-provider seam). The governance architecture
demonstrably works; the remaining gap (evidence grounding) is closable by
configuration, not redesign.

*This campaign evaluated only. No StratAgent code, agents, or prompts were
modified. Findings are not softened; conclusions are scoped to the genuine
evidence gathered (n=3 engagements + 2 baselines).*
