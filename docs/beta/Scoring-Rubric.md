# StratAgent Beta — Scoring Rubric

Two layers: a **participant-facing 7-point rubric** (what beta users score, kept
simple), and the **internal 14-criterion mapping** (how the program lead
aggregates against the research-evaluation rubric for GA comparability).

---

## A. Participant-facing rubric (score each engagement)

Score 1–7. Anchors: **7** = partner-quality, would use as-is after review · **5** =
solid draft, some edits · **3** = weak, major rework · **1** = unusable/wrong.

| # | Dimension | Score it high when… | Score it low when… |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Problem framing** | It found your *real* decision, not the symptom | It solved a different/easier question |
| 2 | **Structure** | Branches are clean, complete, non-overlapping | Gaps, overlaps, or a template recital |
| 3 | **Evidence & assumptions** | Claims trace to data or clearly-labeled assumptions | Floating numbers, hidden assumptions |
| 4 | **Reasoning** | Logic connecting analysis→answer is rigorous, non-obvious | Shallow, generic, or hand-wavy |
| 5 | **Recommendation** | Clear, decisive, with the conditions that would flip it | Vague "it depends" / no real call |
| 6 | **Risk & Challenge** | The Challenger caught something real; risks are honest | Generic risk list; no real stress-test |
| 7 | **Clarity** | Exec-readable, answer up front, no filler | Rambling, buried recommendation |
| 8 | **Calibration** | Stated confidence matched how solid it actually was | Overconfident, or hedged everything |
| — | **Overall** | Would you put this in front of your stakeholder (after review)? | |

Plus the behavioral captures on the [post-engagement form](Evaluation-Forms.md):
time, act/don't-act, numbers overridden, editing effort, completion.

---

## B. How to score fairly

- **Score the system as run** — do not mentally "fix" it first.
- **Separate the recommendation from the reasoning.** A right answer with weak
  support still scores low on Evidence/Reasoning.
- **Judge numbers as assumptions** unless you supplied the data — a plausible but
  unbacked figure is not "good evidence," it's a labeled assumption (that's fine
  for a draft; score Evidence on *whether it's honestly labeled and traceable*,
  not on whether the number is externally verified).
- **Reward the Challenger** when it genuinely surfaces a blindspot or overreach —
  that is the system's core value.

---

## C. Internal 14-criterion mapping (program lead only)

Aggregated to match the [research-evaluation rubric](../reviews/v1.0-Research-Evaluation.md)
so beta scores are comparable to the internal baseline (StratAgent 7.6 / baseline
5.5). Participant dimensions roll up as:

| Research criterion | Fed by participant dimension(s) |
|---|---|
| Problem framing | 1 |
| Framework selection | 2 (+ report inspection) |
| MECE quality | 2 |
| Evidence usage | 3 |
| Strategic reasoning | 4 |
| Financial reasoning | 4 + form Q13 (numbers overridden) |
| Operational reasoning | 4 (ops-archetype engagements) |
| Risk analysis | 6 |
| Recommendation quality | 5 |
| Implementation roadmap | report inspection + form |
| Report clarity | 7 |
| Governance effectiveness | 6 + Challenger perception |
| Confidence calibration | 8 |
| Overall consulting quality | Overall |

**Two scorers per report where feasible** (participant + a program-side reviewer)
so we can report inter-rater agreement and correct for participant leniency. The
gap between participant self-score and reviewer score is itself a metric (naive
over-trust signal).
